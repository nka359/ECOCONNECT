Our Aim

EcoConnect Bridge exists to show how everyday infrastructure can become productive — generating clean energy while serving transport. Our pilot goal is to demonstrate a cost-effective lane-level solar + wireless charging strip with storage, then scale to full bridge corridors.

Goals

  • Deploy an affordable pilot (₹20L–₹30L) to validate tech and user acceptance.
  • Reduce urban transport emissions and improve EV adoption by reducing charging stops.
  • Engage communities — visible green infrastructure increases public support for renewables.
  • Open-source the design so cities can replicate it.
Aim explained — practical, community-driven, and replicable.

Features

Solar Canopies

High-efficiency photovoltaic panels on canopies and rails capture sunlight for live charging and storage.

Wind Micro-Turbines

Vertical-axis micro-turbines along railings capture cross-wind for night/cloudy generation.

Piezoelectric Tiles

Durable tiles in lanes harvest kinetic energy and glow for safety at night.

Wireless Charging Lanes

Inductive coils in road transfer power to vehicle receivers as they pass.

Energy Storage

Batteries & supercapacitors hold energy for immediate supply and smoothing.

Smart Control

AI-managed orchestration balances charging, storage and grid export for efficiency & safety.

Why these features?

The combination provides continuous, distributed generation. Solar and wind capture primary energy; piezo adds consistent kinetic capture; storage and smart control make delivery reliable. Wireless lanes give the highest user-value (reduced stops) but need receiver retrofits in vehicles.

Note: pieces can be phased: start with solar lane + battery + one wireless strip → expand with turbines and piezo tiles.
Features designed for practical deployment and scale.

How the Bridge Works

  1. Collect: PV arrays, micro-turbines & piezo tiles generate DC power.
  2. Condition: Inverters, MPPT, and power electronics condition & harmonize power.
  3. Store: Energy goes into modular battery banks and supercapacitors for fast bursts.
  4. Manage: BMS + EMS (AI logic) prioritizes lane charging, infrastructure needs, or grid export.
  5. Transfer: Inductive coils produce AC magnetic fields; vehicle receivers convert to DC and charge batteries while moving.
  6. Safety & Comm: Sensors, temperature monitors, and OCPP-like comms ensure safe operations and telemetry.

Vehicle requirements

Vehicles need compatible inductive receivers for in-motion charging. Public buses and city fleets can be prioritized for retrofits to maximize benefit early in pilots.

Technical Outputs

  • Real-time energy metrics (kW, kWh), vehicle charge logs.
  • Power exported to local grid or microgrid node.
  • Reduced idling & range-stress for EVs.
  • Community benefits: street-lighting, bus-stop power, emergency backup.
Maintenance: modular panels & replaceable tiles mean lower long-term O&M.
Functions explained — from capture to charge.

Inputs & Outputs

Inputs

  • Sunlight for PV arrays
  • Wind for micro-turbines
  • Vehicle motion for piezoelectric tiles
  • Grid connection for balancing & import/export

Outputs

  • Electricity for wireless charging lanes (kW)
  • Power for bridge systems (lighting, sensors)
  • Surplus export to local grid or community microgrids
  • Telemetry & energy usage data

Expected benefits

Lower city emissions, stronger EV adoption, less strain on centralized grids during peak times, and local resilience with battery backup.

Affordability

Phased rollouts reduce upfront cost. Target first retrofits on high-usage bus lanes and short demonstration bridges to show ROI and gather data.
